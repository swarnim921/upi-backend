# UPI Dashboard System - User Manual

## ⭐ What is the UPI Dashboard System?
The UPI Dashboard System is a secure and easy-to-use platform that allows you to manage your digital payments. Think of it as your personal digital wallet where you can send money to friends, pay merchants, and keep track of all your expenses in one place.

## ⭐ What Can You Do?
- **Register**: Create your own account to start using the system.
- **Login**: Securely access your account.
- **Send Payments**: Transfer money to other users or shop owners instantly.
- **Receive Payments**: Get money from others directly into your account.
- **Refund**: If a transaction goes wrong, you can request a refund.
- **View Transactions**: See a history of everywhere you've spent or received money.

## ⭐ Screens Explained

### 1. Register Flow
**What it is:** The sign-up screen.
**What to do:**
- Enter your **Name**, **Email**, and create a strong **Password**.
- The system will create a unique **UPI ID** for you (like `yourname@upi`).
- Once done, you are ready to log in!

### 2. Login Flow
**What it is:** The entry point to your dashboard.
**What to do:**
- Enter your **Email** and **Password**.
- Click "Login".
- You will be taken to your personal dashboard.

### 3. Payment Flow (Sending Money)
**What it is:** How you pay someone.
**What to do:**
- Click on **"Send Money"**.
- Enter the **UPI ID** of the person you want to pay (e.g., `shopkeeper@upi`).
- Enter the **Amount** (e.g., ₹500).
- Click **"Pay"**.
- You will receive an **OTP** (One-Time Password) on your phone.
- Enter the OTP to confirm.
- **Success!** The money is sent instantly.

### 4. Refund Flow
**What it is:** Getting your money back if something goes wrong.
**What to do:**
- Go to **"Transaction History"**.
- Find the failed or incorrect transaction.
- Click **"Request Refund"**.
- Select a reason (e.g., "Double payment").
- The system will process your request and return the money to your wallet.

### 5. Error Messages Meaning
- **"User not found"**: The email or UPI ID you entered does not exist. Check the spelling.
- **"Insufficient balance"**: You don't have enough money in your wallet to make the payment.
- **"Invalid OTP"**: The code you entered is wrong. Try requesting a new one.

## ⭐ Practical Example: Sending ₹500

Imagine you are at a coffee shop and want to pay the bill of ₹500.

1. **Open the App**: Log in with your email and password.
2. **Start Payment**: Click "Send Money".
3. **Enter Details**: Type the shop's UPI ID (e.g., `coffeehouse@upi`) and enter `500`.
4. **Confirm**: The app asks, "Are you sure?". Click "Yes".
5. **Security Check**: You get a text message with code `123456`. Enter this code in the app.
6. **Done!**: You see a green tick mark saying "Payment Successful". The shopkeeper gets the money immediately.
